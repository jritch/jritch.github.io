• Shows synthetic data of the form y=x, with a smaller sinusoidal trend and random noise.
  • Rotating the data by 90 degrees clockwise, the line of best fit now coincides with the x-axis.
  • Then, we stretch the data along the y-axis, so we can clearly see which data are above and below the trend-line.
  • This allows us to notice the secondary trend in the data.
  • This is equivalent to examining the coordinates of the data after performing Principal Component Analysis.
    •

  • If we wish to compare the values of the different bars, we must compare the lengths of the elements.
  • By re-normalizing the data, we convert a mesure of length to a judgement of position along a common scale.
  • This kind of shifting of the y-axis is useful, but also also perceived as potentially deceptive (outlined in Tufte).
  • By making this a transient, non-destructive transformation, we avoid this criticism.
